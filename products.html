Mailing Letters

In prison there are few things to look forward to and recieving mail is one of them.

Things never do when sending a letter, as it will result in the letter being rejected:

  • Don't use staples or paper clips.
  • Never use marker, crayon, glitter, glue, stickers or lipstick on the letter or envelope.
  • Don't use perfume or any other fragrance.
  • Any drawings or markings that can be misconstrued as secret code etc. will result in refusal.
  • Never write anything in the letters that you wouldn't want a third party to read.
  • (All mail is inspected and read by staff)

Mailing Photos

If there is one thing an Inmate loves more than getting a letter in the mail, it's getting photos.


Some important things to remember:

  • Photos should be no larger than 4"x6"
  • Always print the inmates name and ID number on the back of the photo in pen (this helps the mail room staff)
  • Photos should never contain nudity, sexually suggestive material
  • Hand gestures and tattoos are often not allowed because of gang implications
  • Only 5 photos can be sent in an envelope with a single stamp at a time.
    • and often times a facility will only allow 3-5 photos
  • Remember staff and other inmates will be seeing these

Books, Magazines, and News Papers

Receiving books, magazines or newspapers is a gift that really keeps on giving.


  • Do not send more then 3 books at a time.
  • All books must be new and soft cover (no hardcover or spiral bound)
  • Always ship USPS Use the inmate's name and ID number with the facility mailing address

Holiday/Birthday Cards?


You can send holiday and birthday cards to an inmate.
